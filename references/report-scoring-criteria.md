# Report Scoring Criteria

Guidelines for scoring and writing the 5 evaluated sections of the marketing strategy report.

## The 5 Scored Sections

Each section uses a 5-point scale. Score honestly based on what the market data shows.

| Section | What to Evaluate |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Target Audience** | How clearly defined and reachable is the audience? Are there demand signals in the data? |
| **Pricing Strategy** | What does the market bear? What do similar products charge? What models are validated? |
| **Sales Channels** | What distribution paths are realistically available and how competitive are they? |
| **Promotion Methods** | What organic/paid channels are viable given a small team size and limited budget? |
| **Small-Scale Suitability** | How feasible is this for a solo dev or small team to execute without significant resources? |

## Scoring Honesty

**Do not inflate scores.** If the market data suggests a challenging environment, score it
honestly at 2/5 rather than rounding up to 4/5. Users rely on these scores to make decisions.

For each section, include:
- Star rating (★★★★☆ format) and numeric score (N/5)
- A concrete table of options or benchmarks drawn from the research data
- An explanation of what the score means for this specific product
- The scale definition so the user understands what each level represents

## Promotion Methods: Two Required Tracks

Section 3.4 (Promotion Methods) must always include two parallel tracks:
- **グローバル向け (Global):** Channels from Reddit/HN data (English-language)
- **日本向け (Japan):** Channels from Qiita data (X `#個人開発`, note, はてなブックマーク, AppBank, etc.)

Each track should include a recommended promotion sequence: **pre-launch / launch / post-launch**.

## Writing Style

**Be concrete and specific.** Generic advice is not useful.

| Bad | Good |
|-----|------|
| "post on social media" | "post weekly dev progress on r/IndieDev, targeting Tuesday/Wednesday for best visibility" |
| "consider your pricing" | "F2P with optional ¥480 cosmetics matches the 85% of top-grossing casual mobile games in the dataset" |

Additional guidelines:
- Back every major recommendation with something from the research data
- Acknowledge uncertainty honestly — if data is thin for a niche, say so
- Be action-oriented: end with a clear minimum viable marketing plan
- Do not make recommendations that contradict the data just to sound optimistic
